Demonetization

"Demonetization is just the beginning in fight against black money"

-PM Modi

Download Sample Synopsis


(Last Date of Submission : 27th Feb,17 till 06:00am)

Abstract

With an aim to take strict action against rising corruption, counterfeiting and tax evasion, the Modi government on 8th November 2016 decided that ₹500 and ₹1000 notes will no longer be legal tender. This decision will go down in the annals of history as one of the significant moves made by any leader of India but did this decision turned the fortune of the country towards a better future? It’s doubtful because when 86% of a country’s currency constituting 12.2% of its GDP, is squeezed off the market and sought to be replaced by a new currency, there would obviously be significant consequences of that decision. Though demonetization has helped India to make a move towards becoming cashless and take a leap towards becoming digitally sound (Digital India). But looking at some of the Digital economies around the world like Brazil, USA etc. which are far more corrupt nations than the cash economies like Japan the question arises is demonetization the only way for curbing corruption and black money? Was it really necessary? Has it really eliminated the black money from the country and will this put an end to the corruption in the nation?
