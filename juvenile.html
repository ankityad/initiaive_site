Juvenile Deliquency

"Juvenile crime is the fastest rising crime today"

-Maneka Gandhi

Download Sample Synopsis


(Last Date of Submission : 27th Feb,17 till 06:00am)

Abstract

The greatest wealth and strength of any country is its youth. They are the building blocks of a nation but if that youth will deviates from its path then imagining a bright future about the country becomes questionable. Today juvenile delinquency has become a major issue of concern for our country as the number of crimes conducted by youth went up from 35,465 in 2012 to a higher 42,566 in 2014. Also a data by the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) shows a substantial rise in the crime rate for juveniles between 2010 and 2016. There exist some cases where majority (70.3%) of the children who were serving in the detention centres were unaware about the consequences of their acts, there were also cases recorded where juveniles were found to be repeated offenders of crimes they conducted before. Such figures raise the questions about what can be the possible reasons behind this increment. Can we control it? If yes then how? Is the fast-changing and developing society and multiple expectations from the child led them unintentionally to the wrong way and forced them to do some offensive act or is it their education and surrounding that has builds up their characters this way?
