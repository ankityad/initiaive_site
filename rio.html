India's Breakdown at Rio

"The Indian culture has hindered local sports development"

-China Daily

Download Sample Synopsis


(Last Date of Submission : 27th Feb,17 till 06:00am)

Abstract

After setting a benchmark at the 2012 Olympics where the Indian contingent bagged 6 medals (highest of all the times) the expectations of the fellow countrymen were quite high for the 2016 Rio Olympics but what happened there was out of everyone’s expectations as the contingent of 119 Indian sportsperson who were expected to win at least 10 medals this time had to settle down just with two. Looking closer at the performances of athletes in the events where minimum qualifications were set, revealed that 13 out of the 15 Indian athletes were even not able to meet the benchmark on the basis of which they qualified in the first place. The same athletes who had surpassed Olympic benchmarks, barely a month before the games, fell way below that during the Games. Indian Olympic Association (IOA) officials when asked about the incident said that their representatives were in Rio and would comment later on DNA’s disclosures. Sports Authority of India (SAI) officials also did not respond to calls and texts send to them. With both athletes and the IOA officials looking at the misery silently, various question emerged as who must be blamed for the breakdown? Were the governing bodies not able to provide the players the required amount of support or was as it that the player’s lack of exposure or training which led them to these results remains a mystery.
