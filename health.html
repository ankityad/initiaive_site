Health Prominence

"Healthy India is Wealthy India"

-Arun Jaitley

Download Sample Synopsis


(Last Date of Submission : 27th Feb,17 till 06:00am)

Abstract

The Constitution of India makes the health of the citizens a responsibility of the government. Health Prominence involves providing them with all facilities, better infrastructure, better quality of drugs, instant access to medical care etc irrespective of their economical backgrounds but this has merely became a saying as almost 40% of our country’s population still lacks basic healthcare services and those who have these services available either can’t afford it or it’s too pathetic to have. Today, the condition of the government hospitals is so unacceptable that the infrastructure of hospitals is in a shoddy condition and in some of cases the condition is so worse that they do not even have enough freezers to store the dead bodies. Though, the investment over the health sector has increased over the years (currently 4.7% of its GDP) but it still stands lower than most developed nations where health of the citizen is put as among the first priorities. With various schemes being launched by the present government (Make in India, Skill India) the healthcare sector has somewhere being ignored. The rapid growing population with almost 224 million people living below the poverty line, the healthcare investment in the country has become a major issue of concern and shouldn’t be on the back burner anymore if we want to build a strong and developed nation.
